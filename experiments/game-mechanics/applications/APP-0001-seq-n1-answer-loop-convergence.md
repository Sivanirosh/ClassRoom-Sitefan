# APP-0001 — SEQ-N1 — answer-loop convergence

## Learning target

`SEQ-N1 — Structures décimales`, connecting grouping/conservation and place-value transformations through two related Bip prototypes.

## Adaptation hypothesis

Different stories and mathematical content were expected to make the workshop and console feel like distinct learning experiences.

## Implemented loop

```text
read problem → choose one of three answers → receive feedback → continue
```

The scenarios differed, but the pupil's moment-to-moment action did not.

## Owner findings

- **First owner playtest:** “The scenarios are okay, but the game mechanic is exactly the same.”
- Content coverage, responsive behavior, and technical completion had not exposed this problem.
- The central mechanic needed review before full scene, UX, motion, and asset investment.

No `mechanic approved for expansion` phrase exists because this application predates the revised gate. Do not grant it retroactively.

## Revisions and rejected forms

The answer-card loop was removed as the central interaction:

- EX-0002 moved toward direct reversible organization and exchange.
- EX-0003 moved toward command programming, execution traces, checkpoints, and repair.

## Reusable lessons

- **What failed:** story, visual style, and content differences were treated as mechanic differences.
- **Reuse when:** never as the default central loop; bounded choices may still support a larger direct-manipulation mechanic when they are not the main activity.
- **Avoid when:** the learning relationship can be represented through construction, organization, sequencing, prediction, or debugging.
- **Workflow consequence:** compare verbs, state, challenge, recovery, and moment-to-moment interaction before implementation.

# PRE-0013 — The Search for Planet X

## Why this record exists

This is a strong precedent for making *where and how precisely to inspect* the core decision under a limited information budget.

## Source-backed loop

- **Hidden state:** the solar system is divided into 12 sectors in Standard mode or 18 in Expert mode, with only half in the visible sky.
- **Survey:** a player chooses an object type and a consecutive visible sector range; the app returns how many matching objects occur in that range.
- **Cost trade-off:** surveys of 1–3 sectors cost 4 time, 4–6 cost 3, and Expert-mode surveys of 7–9 cost 2.
- **Precise probe:** targeting one visible sector reveals its object, costs 4 time, and uses one of two target tokens.
- **Persistence/progression:** players record private evidence and later submit theories or attempt a locate action. A failed final claim costs time rather than erasing prior notes.

## Transferable observations

Broad inexpensive samples and narrow expensive samples create a real information-choice economy. Persistent evidence lets a player narrow a model instead of making disconnected guesses.

## Unknowns and cautions

- Exact sub-counts can turn a quantity adaptation into addition rather than estimation.
- The board game’s deduction load, hidden rules, and competitive timing are not evidence of suitability for 6H.
- Uneven distributions can make sample-based inference feel arbitrary unless representativeness is inspectable.

## Copying boundary

Reuse only the abstract trade-off between coarse/cheap and precise/scarce information plus persistent evidence. Do not copy astronomy, circular sectors, object rules, action names, time-track presentation, note sheets, app flow, iconography, or the distinctive locate condition.

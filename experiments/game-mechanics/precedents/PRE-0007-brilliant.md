# PRE-0007 — Brilliant

## Why this record exists

Brilliant is a platform precedent for action-first interactive learning. It is useful at the level of learning-loop principles, not as a screen or content template.

## Source-backed loop

- **Lesson composition:** official materials describe short explanations combined with hands-on problems, immediate feedback, and visual simulations or animations.
- **Contextual help:** Brilliant describes its Koji tutor as using the current problem state and prior actions to guide thinking step by step.
- **Progression:** Learning Paths recommend an order from foundations toward applied problem solving and include practice checkpoints.
- **Motivation:** streak and XP systems track activity and participation; they are not documented mastery measures.
- **Teacher signals:** documented views include active learning time, lesson status, completion, assignments, and course progress.

## Transferable observations

An original activity can interleave brief orientation with direct manipulation, make consequences visible, and provide state-aware hints. Checkpoints can revisit prerequisites without turning every interaction into detached multiple choice.

These first-party descriptions do not independently prove learning effectiveness or reveal Brilliant's adaptive rules.

## Unknowns and cautions

- Do not claim independent achievement, retention, or transfer evidence from first-party product descriptions.
- “Adaptive” does not expose a reproducible learner model or decision algorithm.
- XP, streaks, time, and completion are engagement signals, not mastery evidence.
- Feature availability and behavior may change.

Do not copy problems, lesson sequences, explanations, hints, tutor language, visual assets, animations, screenshots, UI layouts, branding, or distinctive screen composition. Create original curriculum content, state models, copy, feedback, art direction, and progression.

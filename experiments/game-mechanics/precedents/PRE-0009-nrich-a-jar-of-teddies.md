# PRE-0009 — NRICH: A Jar of Teddies

## Why this record exists

This task is a direct precedent for revealing *methods and structure* between an initial quantity estimate and exact ground truth.

## Source-backed loop

- **Prompt:** several views show one filled jar and ask for an estimate of the number of objects.
- **Methods:** optional reveals describe estimates based on color-group ranges, visible layers, a side-view projection, and object/container volume.
- **Ground truth:** the exact total is hidden until after the estimate and method exploration.
- **Extension:** follow-up questions ask about a container for 100 objects and a classroom-scale estimate with larger objects.
- **Recovery:** alternative models let a learner reconsider an unsupported estimate; the page does not document adaptive diagnosis or automated retries.

## Transferable observations

After commitment, reveal one explanatory layer at a time—groups, layers, or a benchmark unit—before the exact total. Multiple defensible models can make the source of estimation error inspectable without reducing the activity to right/wrong feedback.

## Unknowns and cautions

- The page does not establish which strategy pupils choose independently or whether the activity improves transfer.
- Occlusion and packing can make a digital adaptation feel arbitrary unless exact ground truth becomes auditable.
- The source does not document a timed display or game economy.

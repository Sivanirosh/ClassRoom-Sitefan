# PRE-0012 — DragonBox Numbers

## Why this record exists

DragonBox Numbers is a precedent for treating a known magnitude as a manipulable object that can be composed, compared, and revised.

## Source-backed loop

- **Representation:** official product material describes Nooms as characters representing numbers 1–10 and as digital manipulatives inspired by Cuisenaire and Montessori rods.
- **Activities:** the product contains four activities intended to build flexible number understanding rather than simple fact memorization.
- **Manipulation:** publisher-controlled descriptions name stacking, slicing, combining, sorting, and comparing as available actions.
- **Recovery:** pieces remain manipulable and recomposable; the inspected pages do not document a complete failure/retry model for each activity.

## Transferable observations

A known-size reference can be embodied as a repeatable unit. Composing several references beside an unknown magnitude can expose shortfall, excess, and equivalence before a symbolic answer is entered.

## Unknowns and cautions

- The documented values and audience are younger than the `NUM-02` target; the character treatment must not be transplanted.
- Length or area is not automatically proportional to the cardinality of an irregular collection. A collection adaptation must control or explicitly compare density.
- First-party pages do not establish transfer to quantity estimation or 6H learning effectiveness.

Reuse only the abstract idea of composable known-magnitude units and visible mismatch. Do not copy Noom characters, colors/proportions, faces, sounds, animations, activity names, story, screen composition, or interaction presentation.

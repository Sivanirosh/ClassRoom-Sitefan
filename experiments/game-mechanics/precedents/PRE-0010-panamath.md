# PRE-0010 — Panamath

## Why this record exists

Panamath is a narrowly useful component precedent for preventing exact one-by-one counting through controlled brief exposure. It is not a precedent for the complete `NUM-02` estimate/range/reveal loop.

## Source-backed loop

- **Display:** the official online instructions show random mixed-size blue and yellow circles for 600 ms after an unlimited-time practice.
- **Decision:** the participant chooses which color had more circles using one of two keys and is told to make a best guess when uncertain.
- **Constraint:** circle sizes are mixed, and the instructions explicitly say to decide from number rather than size.
- **Progression/recovery:** repeated trials are documented; the inspected instructions do not expose exact counts, numeric intervals, explanatory grouping, or a revision of the same trial.

## Transferable observations

A temporary scene can block exact counting and force a committed approximate judgment. For learning design, that rhythm must be paired with inspectable evidence and non-speed-dependent access; otherwise it tests transient perception rather than reference-based estimation.

## Unknowns and cautions

- This is assessment-like and binary, not a collection-estimation lesson.
- A fixed 600 ms exposure may measure visual processing, attention, or reaction behavior that `NUM-02` must not treat as mastery.
- The cited page does not support claims about numeric-estimate calibration or exact reveal.

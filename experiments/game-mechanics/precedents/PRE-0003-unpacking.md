# PRE-0003 — Unpacking

## Why this record exists

Unpacking offers a precedent for making classification and fit legible through objects and environments rather than a prose-first question shell.

## Source-backed loop

- **Player verbs:** remove possessions from boxes, inspect them, place them in rooms, arrange spaces, and move among rooms.
- **Constraint:** placement validity depends on contextual interpretation of the object and environment.
- **Flexibility:** approximately correct interpretation can be sufficient for placement, allowing multiple arrangements within meaningful constraints.
- **Feedback/recovery:** contextual validity is part of the puzzle, and objects can be rearranged; the cited first-party text does not fully document invalid-placement signaling, undo, or exact completion rules.

## Transferable observations

A representation can communicate through affordance and context. Learning objects may support more than one visually different arrangement while still enforcing the mathematical relationship that matters.

## Unknowns and cautions

- Do not infer exact UI feedback or recovery controls from these sources.
- Decorative object recognition can become accidental difficulty; educational objects need unambiguous values and accessible text alternatives.
- Open placement should not obscure exact quantities or valid-answer rules.

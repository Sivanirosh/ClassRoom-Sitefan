# PRE-0002 — Wilmot's Warehouse

## Why this record exists

This precedent shows how organization can be player-authored and then tested by use rather than graded against one required taxonomy.

## Source-backed loop

- **Player verbs:** slide, sort, stack, organize, locate, and deliver products.
- **Structure:** products arrive in a jumble; the player chooses an organizational system; later requests test whether objects can be found.
- **Progression:** fast retrieval earns performance stars used for warehouse upgrades.
- **Recovery:** the reviewed first-party pages do not specify failure thresholds, undo, or restart behavior.

## Transferable observations

Letting a pupil choose a representation or grouping can create agency when a later task tests whether the structure is useful. The challenge comes from maintaining and retrieving meaning, not from selecting a teacher-authored label.

Timed retrieval is not automatically suitable for learning; pressure should be removed when it would measure speed or motor fluency instead of the target concept.

## Unknowns and cautions

- Detailed timing and failure rules are not established by the sources below.
- Performance stars are an engagement/progression device, not evidence of conceptual mastery.
- A classroom version needs explicit non-timed alternatives.

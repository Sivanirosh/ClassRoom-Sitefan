# PRE-0006 — Into the Breach

## Why this record exists

This precedent grounds deterministic preview: show what will happen, then challenge the player to alter an ordered set of consequences with limited actions.

## Source-backed loop

- **Player verbs:** control units, inspect announced enemy attacks, counter them, and protect civilian buildings.
- **Constraint:** combat is turn-based and several visible threats compete for limited intervention.
- **Preview:** enemy attacks are telegraphed before they resolve.
- **Run recovery:** after defeat, help can be sent into another timeline for a new attempt.
- **Unknown detail:** the official high-level page does not establish exact action economy, undo limits, grid rules, or resolution calculations.

## Transferable observations

A preview can turn feedback into planning information before commitment. Ordered checkpoints are especially useful when otherwise commutative actions would create false strategic depth.

## Unknowns and cautions

- Do not infer exact ordering or undo rules from the cited product page.
- Too many simultaneous threats can overload a pupil.
- A learning adaptation should make the relevant consequence trace explicit and avoid combat framing when inappropriate.

# PRE-0011 — The Math Learning Center: Number Frames

## Why this record exists

Number Frames is a strong precedent for making known-size groups and benchmark quantities spatially inspectable instead of counting every object one by one.

## Source-backed loop

- **Representation:** the app provides five-, ten-, double-ten-, hundred-, and custom frames.
- **Actions:** learners place counters and may drag single counters or stacks of 5 and 10 into frames or the workspace.
- **Purpose:** the publisher says frames support counting, representing, comparing, and computing and help pupils see equal groups in relation to benchmark quantities.
- **Recovery:** it is documented as an open-ended manipulative, not a scored sequence; counters can be moved and restructured rather than locked after a wrong answer.

## Transferable observations

Known-capacity frames make full groups and remainders visible. For estimation, a reference frame can support a pre-count bracket, while exact repacking can be reserved for the later verification stage.

## Unknowns and cautions

- The source describes a primary-grade manipulative, not a 6H estimation game.
- If a system automatically fills frames before commitment, the activity becomes exact counting and bypasses estimation.
- The source does not document a challenge progression, error model, or interval commitment.

## Learning objective

## Prerequisites

## Misconceptions and productive difficulty

Document likely wrong models, shortcuts, and sources of confusion. Explain which difficulty is educationally useful and which is accidental interface friction.

## Evidence of learning

Describe what actions or explanations would demonstrate learning beyond obtaining the correct answer.

## Core interaction hypothesis

> If the learner repeatedly **[manipulates X]** while receiving **[specific feedback]**, then **[target relationship]** becomes visible and usable.

## Exercise loop

1. Present a focused situation.
2. Let the learner manipulate or predict.
3. Make consequences visible.
4. Ask for a decision or explanation.
5. Provide specific feedback and one next action.

## Progression

Describe how representation, numbers, vocabulary, distractors, or support change across applicable HarmoS years. Do not use speed alone as difficulty.

## Feedback and scaffolding

Specify immediate feedback, optional hints, error recovery, and how support fades. Never reduce feedback to only “correct” or “incorrect.”

## Content model

Document generated values, authored scenarios, valid answer rules, edge cases, and how impossible or ambiguous questions are prevented.

## Interaction states

List the minimum states and transitions: introduction, active manipulation, submitted answer, feedback, continuation, and completion where relevant.

## Accessibility and devices

Cover keyboard, touch target size, non-color cues, readable motion, reduced-motion behavior, language clarity, and any necessary alternative representation.

## Prototype boundaries

### Included

- One learning objective
- One complete exercise loop
- Enough variation to test the hypothesis

### Excluded

- World maps, economies, avatars, collaboration, production persistence, and unrelated progression systems

## Observation plan

Record what to observe, what would support the hypothesis, what would falsify it, and which learner comments or actions matter.

## Findings and decisions

Append dated observations. Preserve rejected decisions and their reasons rather than rewriting history.

## Promotion criteria

State the evidence required before extracting this work into the classroom application.

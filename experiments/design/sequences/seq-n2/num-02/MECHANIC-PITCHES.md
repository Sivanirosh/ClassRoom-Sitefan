## Pitch A — Fenêtre calibrée

- **Pitch ID / target:** `N2-P1`; `NUM-02`.
- **Precedents:** [[PRE-0008-estimation-180]], [[PRE-0009-nrich-a-jar-of-teddies]], [[PRE-0010-panamath]].
- **Mechanic patterns:** [[MEC-0005-commit-reveal-recalibrate]], supported by [[MEC-0004-action-first-interactive-learning]].
- **Extracted pattern:** the pupil deliberately opens a bounded view of a collection, then the scene closes before exact counting. They choose the known reference that best anchors what they saw, commit lower/upper bounds, and receive a staged grouping reveal before one revision.
- **Primary pupil verbs:** open, inspect, choose a reference, bracket, reveal evidence, revise, explain.
- **Core loop:**

```text
trigger a global view
→ compare the remembered structure with labeled reference collections
→ commit lower and upper bounds
→ reveal benchmark groups/tens in stages
→ move one bound or defend both
→ transfer with changed clustering
```

- **Visible state representation:** a closed/open observation window; two or three labeled reference collections; lower/upper handles on an ordered band; ghosted original interval; the same unknown collection progressively regrouped into tens/reference chunks.
- **Source of challenge:** form a global quantity judgment before one-by-one counting, then decide which reference and how much uncertainty are defensible.
- **Meaningful decisions:** which reference is closest; whether a replay is worth using; how wide the interval should be; which bound new evidence should move.
- **Mistake information and recovery:** the reveal first outlines one known-size chunk, then shows all full groups plus remainder. Overflow below the lower bound or unused capacity at the upper bound points to the conflicting claim. The original interval remains visible and one revision is allowed before the exact total appears.
- **Concept-native fit:** temporary concealment preserves estimation as different from exact counting; selecting a reference and constructing two bounds directly expresses the checkpoint. The mechanic fails if the “flash” becomes the main skill.
- **Sequence-spine fit and downstream handoff:** strongest continuity with the roadmap’s provisional `Fenêtre-éclair` beat. It produces an explicit ordered interval and an exact grouped total for `NUM-05`, but the brief exposure itself has no downstream value and must not become the sequence’s recurring gimmick.
- **Three-scene graybox possibility:**
  1. **Orientation:** unlimited learner-triggered view; compare a 50-reference with one collection, set generous bounds, then inspect tens.
  2. **Complication:** one optional replay and two plausible references; clustering makes the first global impression unreliable, so staged grouping evidence should move only one bound.
  3. **Independence:** choose a reference and interval without a suggested range; use either brief-view or persistent accessible mode; explain the revision from the reveal.
- **Accessibility and implementation risks:** high risk of testing visual memory/attention. No automatic start, speed score, or mandatory 600 ms exposure. The graybox must include a persistent/replayable evidence mode that preserves the reference decision. Dense collections, animation, and hidden screen-reader totals can invalidate the task.
- **What is deliberately not copied:** no Panamath colors, timings, dot generator, binary test, or assessment claim; no Estimation 180/NRICH images, prompts, values, methods, page choreography, or branding.
- **Kill criterion:** reject this direction if owner replay shows that success depends mainly on exposure duration or recall, or if the reference remains decorative and arbitrary bounds work just as well.

## Pitch B — Gabarits de collection **(recommended)**

- **Pitch ID / target:** `N2-P2`; `NUM-02`.
- **Precedents:** [[PRE-0011-number-frames]], [[PRE-0012-dragonbox-numbers]], [[PRE-0009-nrich-a-jar-of-teddies]].
- **Mechanic patterns:** [[MEC-0006-benchmark-chunking-and-bracketing]], with the before/after trace from [[MEC-0005-commit-reveal-recalibrate]] and direct manipulation guardrails from [[MEC-0001-constrained-spatial-organization]].
- **Extracted pattern:** the pupil selects a known-size collection gabarit (for example 10, 50, or 100), composes a small number of translucent copies beside—not automatically filled by—the unknown collection, and constructs a lower and upper plan from “this many references fit; the next would exceed”. Exact regrouping becomes available only after commitment.
- **Primary pupil verbs:** select a reference, place/compose, compare, bracket, reject an invalid reference, regroup, verify.
- **Core loop:**

```text
inspect an unknown collection and known-size gabarits
→ choose and compose a reference without moving individual unknown objects
→ mark the last defensible lower chunk and first defensible upper chunk
→ commit the resulting interval
→ regroup the real collection into tens/reference chunks
→ inspect shortfall/remainder and revise
```

- **Visible state representation:** one original collection; labeled 10/50/100 gabarit cards containing the same unit; a bounded number of reference copies; two “capacity plans” for lower and upper claims; then an auditable full-groups-plus-remainder reveal.
- **Source of challenge:** choose an informative unit and reason multiplicatively/additively from full benchmark chunks plus a visible remainder without converting the activity into exact placement.
- **Meaningful decisions:** which benchmark size balances clarity and effort; how many copies plausibly fit; whether object size/density makes a candidate reference valid; whether the remainder justifies a tighter upper or higher lower bound.
- **Mistake information and recovery:** a poor gabarit exposes visible mismatch or incompatible unit/density before commitment and can be replaced. After commitment, the exact regrouping shows full groups and remainder, identifying which bound failed. The pupil edits the plan rather than restarting.
- **Concept-native fit:** the central action is using a *collection repère* to create an interval. It directly turns `NUM-01` exact groups into `NUM-02` approximate units while preserving estimation before verification.
- **Sequence-spine fit and downstream handoff:** strongest handoff in the set. Bounds arise as exact multiples/remainders of known groups, and the revealed total stays connected to its numeral. `NUM-05` can immediately compare the lower, exact, and upper values by place.
- **Three-scene graybox possibility:**
  1. **Orientation:** choose a 50-gabarit; one copy clearly fits and two would exceed, so build a 50–100 interval before revealing tens plus remainder.
  2. **Complication:** two visually tempting gabarits use different object size/density; reject the invalid comparison, choose the comparable reference, and repair a bound after grouped reveal.
  3. **Independence:** choose among 10/50/100 for a moderately clustered collection, compose no more than three references, commit bounds, then verify and explain why the chosen unit was useful.
- **Accessibility and implementation risks:** no timer and low memory burden, but draggable overlays can create motor/vision barriers. Provide select-then-place and keyboard controls. Never imply equal area means equal cardinality: use verified identical units and controlled density, and make invalid references explicitly rejectable. Do not auto-snap every unknown object or expose exact occupancy.
- **What is deliberately not copied:** no Math Learning Center frames/toolbars/counters, no DragonBox characters/colors/sounds/proportions, no NRICH images/values/method text. Gabarits, units, scene data, controls, and feedback are original.
- **Kill criterion:** reject this direction if pupils can derive an exact count by mechanical filling before estimating, or if footprint/density ambiguity makes a reasonable interval look wrong.

**Why recommended:** this direction makes the required reference collection—not timing, recall, or an added investigation economy—the source of play. It also creates the cleanest conceptual bridge from `NUM-01` grouping to `NUM-05` exact order while leaving room for meaningful complications about reference validity and remainder.

## Pitch C — Bandes d’enquête

- **Pitch ID / target:** `N2-P3`; `NUM-02`.
- **Precedents:** [[PRE-0013-the-search-for-planet-x]], [[PRE-0014-desmos-fit-fights]].
- **Mechanic patterns:** [[MEC-0007-budgeted-sampling-and-interval-narrowing]], with [[MEC-0005-commit-reveal-recalibrate]] and [[MEC-0004-action-first-interactive-learning]].
- **Extracted pattern:** most of the collection remains behind contiguous shutters. The pupil spends a small observation budget on broad or narrow bands, compares each glimpse with a known reference sample, preserves an evidence log, and tightens lower/upper bounds before deciding when to commit.
- **Primary pupil verbs:** choose where to look, sample, compare with a reference, record evidence, narrow, commit, request one recovery probe.
- **Core loop:**

```text
inspect the concealed whole and reference sample
→ choose a broad/cheap or narrow/precise observation band
→ receive a brief but persistent evidence card
→ narrow lower/upper bounds
→ spend another observation or commit
→ see which evidence supports/conflicts with the interval
```

- **Visible state representation:** 6–8 concealed contiguous zones; two or three observation tokens; broad/narrow window costs; evidence cards showing window size and a non-exact density/reference comparison; a number band whose impossible outer regions shrink while original bounds remain traced.
- **Source of challenge:** acquire useful and representative evidence with limited observations rather than passively receiving the collection.
- **Meaningful decisions:** where to sample; broad versus precise window; whether two distant samples are needed; whether current evidence justifies narrowing; when another observation is worth more than committing.
- **Mistake information and recovery:** each sample persists rather than returning correct/incorrect. At commitment, a contextual capacity model shows which sampled region strains one bound without revealing the exact total. One extra probe enables revision; final grouped reveal follows afterward.
- **Concept-native fit:** estimation genuinely uses partial evidence and references, but the information-acquisition economy adds a second concept. It must remain concrete and cannot drift into informal probability or hidden-rule deduction.
- **Sequence-spine fit and downstream handoff:** produces the richest visible interval history for `NUM-05` and `NUM-06`. However, its sampling vocabulary is not part of the N2 spine and could dilute the simpler reference → bound relationship.
- **Three-scene graybox possibility:**
  1. **Orientation:** one suggested broad band and a 50-reference; add the evidence card and tighten a generous interval.
  2. **Complication:** an uneven collection makes one edge sample misleading; choose a second distant band and preserve both pieces of evidence before revising.
  3. **Independence:** spend three tokens across broad/narrow bands, commit an interval, inspect one bound conflict, and choose one recovery probe.
- **Accessibility and implementation risks:** highest cognitive and implementation load. Too many tokens/marks can obscure estimation. Brief peeks need persistent evidence cards and untimed access. Exact sub-counts would collapse the loop into addition; vague density labels could make feedback arbitrary. Uneven distributions require authored representativeness cues.
- **What is deliberately not copied:** no astronomy, sectors, target tokens, time track, hidden-object rules, map, or locate condition from Planet X; no Desmos meter, residual graphics, class overlay, lesson data, wording, or UI.
- **Kill criterion:** reject this direction if pupils spend more reasoning on token rules and sampling strategy than on quantity references and bounds, or if different reasonable samples receive inconsistent verdicts.
